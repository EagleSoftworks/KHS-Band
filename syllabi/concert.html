Social Media

"Think once before you act, twice before you speak, and three times before you post on Facebook."

Band members are expected to be respectful of each other both inside and outside of class. Therefore, ONLY POSITIVE COMMENTS about the band, music students, or the staff can be posted on social media. If you have concerns or complaints, please make an appointment to speak with Dr. Davison. All concerns will be handled professionally and with improvement in mind. If we don't know there is a problem, we can't make the situation better.


Back to School Clothes

Please be sure your son or daughter owns a white, button-down dress shirt (long or short sleeve), black crew-length socks, and ALL black shoes or sneakers. Students will use these for every full uniform performance. Students will also receive an order form for the required pep band shirts. Please contact Dr. Davison if you are unable to afford these items and confidential arrangements will be made to provide them.


Equipment

Every band member is responsible for the proper care and use of KHS property. Students should only play or touch their own instruments or instruments that have been assigned to them. Only percussionists may play on percussion equipment. Students provide their own accessories for their instruments, i.e., reeds swabs, valve oil, mutes, etc. Drummers provide their own sticks. Please contact Dr. Davison if you are unable to afford these items and confidential arrangements will be made to provide them.


Loaner instruments

Loaner instruments are available for a small fee. Students must complete a loaner contract before an instrument will be assigned. Instruments are loaned on a first come, first served basis and are available while supplies last.


Buying Instruments

Quality musical instruments really do make a difference. There are a lot of pretty looking instruments out there that are substandard. If you are planning to buy an instrument, PLEASE contact Dr. Davison to help insure your money is well-spent.


Performances

Concert Band performances are a group effort and therefore everyone must be present at all required performances. Performances are Common Assessments.


Excused Absences

Bring in a note from a parent or guardian at least 2 weeks in advance of the performance.

Excused absences include illness, death in the family, religious observance, or unavoidably scheduled family commitments, and emergencies.Except for emergencies or illness, two weeks, advanced, written notice must be given by a PARENT OR GUARDIAN. In cases of emergency or illness, please provide a note on the first day back in school.Students with an excused absence must complete a make-up assignment. A list of choices will be provided. Failure to complete the assignment will result in a 0 for the performance.


Unexcused absences include but are not limited to transportation, work, babysitting, parties, dances, dates, haircuts, oversleeping, and forgetfulness. An unexcused absence will be averaged as a zero. Should a school related conflict arise, it will be handled on an individual basis with the advisor of the activity and Dr. Davison.


Band and Sports

We want students to participate in both. The Athletic and Music Department work closely to insure most performances and games do not conflict. Occasionally, students may need parents to drive them back to school after a game in time for performances. Dr. Davison will help coordinate carpools. On the rare occasion that students cannot do both, freshman and JV players are expected to be with the band. Varsity players can choose. Players who miss a performance due to a sport's conflict are expected to substitute another performance scheduled outside of their sports' seasons. Dr. Davison will work with students individually on this requirement.

Academic Expectations

  1. Mastery of assigned literature.
  2. Satisfactory playing grades and practice results.
  3. Acceptable behavior and esprit de corps.
  4. Attendance at rehearsals, performances, lessons, etc.

Classroom Behavior Expectations

  1. Be respectful - "If you don't have anything nice to say, don't say anything at all."
  2. Be responsible
  3. Participate

Competencies

  1. Creating - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.
  2. Performing - Realizing artistic ideas and work through interpretation and presentation.
  3. Responding - Understanding and evaluating how the arts convey meaning.
  4. Connecting - Relating artistic ideas and work with personal meaning and external context.

Course Assignments

  • Common Assessments (tests, daily playing competencies, performances, etc.) 60%
  • Formative Assessments (worksheets, misc. assignments, etc.) 10%
  • Creative Process (working collaboratively, practicing during class) 30%

Report Card Weights

Each semester counts for 40% of the grade. The final exam is 20% of the yearlong grade.


Personally-Owned Instruments

Keep a record of serial numbers on file at home for insurance purposes.

Check with your homeowner's policy for coverage.

  • The school is not responsible for lost, stolen, or damaged instruments owned by individual students.
  • Please put your name on the outside of the case. A luggage tag works well.
  • Keep instruments away from water, unless cleaning brass instruments, and extreme heat or cold. Be sure to clean your instrument properly and regularly.
  • You will be issued a locker with lock for security purposes.

Part Assignment

Students are assigned to parts based on ability and grade level. Competencies are leveled according to class standing (freshman, sophomore, juniors, and seniors). Students may challenge each other to move to a new part. Challenge procedures are available upon request. Percussions are expected to demonstrate competency on all percussion instruments.


Private Lessons

Private lessons are strongly encouraged. Individual instruction is the most effective and efficient way to quickly gain added proficiency on your instrument. Please see Dr. Davison if you need a recommendation for a private teacher.


Advanced Credit

Students may contract for Advanced Credit in Concert Band. Requirements will be explained when contracts are handed out before the end of first quarter.


Awards

Students earn Awards Points throughout their years in Concert Band towards the following levels of achievement: Certificate, Varsity Letter, Academic Pin, Pewter Pen, and Crystal Eagle. The awards structure will be explained during class. Awards Points are tallied throughout each year and awards are presented at the end of each year.


Band Buddies

All newbies will be assigned an experienced band member as their Band Buddy. Band Buddies help the new students with everything from reminders about performance needs to how to find a math classroom to where the bathrooms are located.
