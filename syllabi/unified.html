What is Unified Music?

Unified Music is part of the General Music strand of the SAU 9 Music Curriculum. It first became part of the Program of Studies for the 2014-2015 school year. This means that students receive a variety of music experiences rather than focus on type of music group, such as band or chorus. This class hopes to integrate Job Prep students with students who are enrolled in regular education classes and thereby provide academic and social growth to everyone involved.


Academic Expectations

  1. Mastery of assigned literature.
  2. Satisfactory playing grades and practice results.
  3. Acceptable behavior and esprit de corps.
  4. Attendance at rehearsals, performances, lessons, etc.

Classroom Behavior Expectations

  1. Be respectful - "If you don't have anything nice to say, don't say anything at all."
  2. Be responsible
  3. Participate

Competencies

  1. Creating - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.
  2. Performing - Realizing artistic ideas and work through interpretation and presentation.
  3. Responding - Understanding and evaluating how the arts convey meaning.
  4. Connecting - Relating artistic ideas and work with personal meaning and external context.

Course Assignments

  • Common Assessments (tests, daily playing competencies, performances, etc.) 60%
  • Formative Assessments (worksheets, misc. assignments, etc.) 10%
  • Creative Process (working collaboratively, practicing during class) 30%

Report Card Weights

Each semester counts for 40% of the grade. The final exam is 20% of the yearlong grade.


Units of Study:

  • African-Style Drumming
  • Music Literacy
  • Instruments of the Orchestra and Band
  • Music Listening

Equipment

Every student is responsible for the proper care and use of KHS property. Students are not allowed to play instruments that do not belong or have not been assigned to them.


Performance

There may be an opportunity for us to perform during the school day. The decision is a collaborative one and is made each semester.
