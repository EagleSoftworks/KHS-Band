Course Description

Instrumental Music Workshop is an exciting opportunity to learn a new band instrument, play your violin, or improve current instrument skills in a more individualized setting when compared to Concert Band or KHS Drumline. Weekly practice assignments, written tests, and in-class performances serve as formative and common assessments. Students must be willing and able to practice at home. A limited number of school owned instruments are available. They will be assigned on a first come-first served basis. Rental instruments are available locally. This course is limited to 18 students.


Class Expectations

This class is different from other classes because the teacher does not tell the student what to work on (unless the student cannot create a project). Students create their own projects to meet the Key Learning Targets with guidance and approval from the teacher. Students must make progress on their music for Formative Assessment grades. Completed projects serve as Common Assessment grades.


Materials Needed for Class:

  • Notebook
  • Folder
  • Pencil
  • Music Book related to projects - borrowed from KHS or student-owned
  • Instrument - a limited number of school-owned instruments are available

Academic Expectations

  1. Mastery of assigned literature.
  2. Satisfactory playing grades and practice results.
  3. Acceptable behavior and esprit de corps.
  4. Attendance at rehearsals, performances, lessons, etc.

Classroom Behavior Expectations

  1. Be respectful - "If you don't have anything nice to say, don't say anything at all."
  2. Be responsible
  3. Participate

Competencies

  1. Creating - Conceiving and developing new artistic ideas and work.
  2. Performing - Realizing artistic ideas and work through interpretation and presentation.
  3. Responding - Understanding and evaluating how the arts convey meaning.
  4. Connecting - Relating artistic ideas and work with personal meaning and external context.

Course Assignments

  • Common Assessments (tests, daily playing competencies, performances, etc.) 60%
  • Formative Assessments (worksheets, misc. assignments, etc.) 10%
  • Creative Process (working collaboratively, practicing during class) 30%

Report Card Weights

Each semester counts for 40% of the grade. The final exam is 20% of the yearlong grade.
